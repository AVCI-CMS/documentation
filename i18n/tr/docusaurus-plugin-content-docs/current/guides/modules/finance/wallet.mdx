# Wallet (Digital Balances)

**Menu Path:** `Finance & Logistics > Wallet`

The **Wallet** module is a central financial ledger where the digital balances of your platform, customers, and merchants are tracked. In a marketplace environment, the Wallet is the most critical component for managing cash flow, merchant earnings, and store credits.

## Understanding Wallet Types

Depending on your platform's structure, wallets can belong to:
- **Customers (Store Credit):** Users can have a wallet balance funded by gift cards, refunds, or loyalty rewards, which they can spend at checkout.
- **Merchants (Earnings):** Sellers accumulate funds in their wallet as their products are sold. This balance represents what the platform owes them, minus commissions.
- **Master Wallet:** The platform's main account showing total retained earnings (commissions + fees).

## Navigating the Wallet Dashboard

When you open the Wallet module, you will see a high-level summary of funds across the system.

### 1. Balance Overview
- **Available Balance:** Funds that are cleared and ready to be used or withdrawn.
- **Pending/Blocked Balance:** Funds that are held temporarily. For instance, a merchant's earnings might be held in escrow for 14 days (the return period) before moving to "Available".

### 2. Payouts (Withdrawal Requests)
Merchants can request to transfer their *Available Balance* to their real bank accounts.
- **Reviewing Requests:** Under the "Payouts" tab, you will see pending withdrawal requests.
- **Approving a Payout:** Click on a pending request, verify the merchant's IBAN or connected Stripe account, and mark it as "Approved" or "Processed". The system will deduct the amount from their wallet.

### 3. Manual Adjustments
Administrators can manually credit or debit a wallet.
- Click **"Add Transaction"**.
- Select the Target Wallet (e.g., a specific customer).
- Enter the Amount and the Type (e.g., "Credit").
- **Reason/Note:** Always leave a descriptive note (e.g., "Customer compensation for delayed shipping") for auditing purposes.

> **Auditing:** The Wallet screen shows current balances. To see the step-by-step history of *how* a wallet reached its current balance, you must use the [Transactions](./transactions.mdx) module.
