# Disputes

**Menu Path:** `Finance & Logistics > Disputes`

The **Disputes** module is the central command for handling customer conflicts. A dispute is usually opened when a customer wants to return an item, claims they never received a package, or initiates a chargeback with their credit card company.

## Understanding the Dispute Lifecycle

1. **Initiation:** A customer opens a dispute from their "My Orders" page, stating a reason (e.g., "Item damaged in transit") and optionally uploading photos.
2. **Review:** The dispute appears in **Finance & Logistics > Disputes** with a status of *OPEN*. 
3. **Communication:** Both the Customer and the Merchant (or Admin) can leave messages and evidence within the dispute thread.
4. **Resolution:** 
   - *Accepted:* The merchant accepts the return. The system instructs the customer to ship the item back. Once received, the Admin processes a *REFUND* via the Payments module.
   - *Rejected:* The merchant refuses the return (e.g., the return window expired). 
   - *Admin Escalation:* If the merchant and customer cannot agree, the Platform Admin steps in as an arbitrator to make a final decision.

## Managing a Dispute

1. Go to **Finance & Logistics > Disputes**.
2. Click on a Dispute ID to open the details.
3. Review the **Related Order**, the **Customer's Claim**, and the **Uploaded Evidence**.
4. Use the internal messaging system to ask the customer for more details.
5. Update the **Dispute Status** to *RESOLVED*, *REFUNDED*, or *REJECTED* based on the outcome.
