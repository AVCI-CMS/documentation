# Transactions

**Menu Path:** `Finance & Logistics > Transactions`

The **Transactions** module acts as the internal financial ledger of your platform. While *Payments* record money moving between the customer's bank and your payment gateway, *Transactions* record how that money is split internally within your platform.

This is especially critical for Marketplace models, where a single payment might generate multiple internal transactions (e.g., Merchant A's earning, Merchant B's earning, and the Platform's commission).

## Understanding Transactions

1. Go to **Finance & Logistics > Transactions**.
2. Click on a Transaction ID to view the details.

### 1. Transaction Types
- **EARNING:** Money credited to a Merchant's account for a successful sale.
- **COMMISSION:** Money deducted from a sale and credited to the Platform as revenue.
- **PAYOUT:** Money transferred from the Merchant's digital wallet to their actual bank account.
- **REFUND / CHARGEBACK:** Money deducted due to a returned item or disputed charge.
- **PENALTY:** Administrative fines deducted from a Merchant's balance.

### 2. Transaction Statuses & Payout Dates
- **PENDING:** The transaction is logged, but the money is not yet available to the merchant. *Example: An order is shipped but not yet delivered. The funds are held in escrow.*
  - *Estimated Payout Date:* If pending, a yellow banner will show the estimated date the funds will be cleared (e.g., "Estimated Transfer: 15 Oct 2026").
- **COMPLETED:** The money has been cleared and officially added to the Merchant's withdrawable Wallet balance.
- **CANCELLED:** The transaction was voided (e.g., the order was cancelled before fulfillment).

### 3. Financial Breakdown
- **Amount:** The exact value of the transaction.
- **Merchant:** Which merchant this transaction belongs to.
- **Description:** An auto-generated explanation (e.g., "Earnings for Order #12345").

*Note:* All transactions are immutable. To correct a mistake, a new counter-transaction (like a refund) must be issued rather than editing the original record.
